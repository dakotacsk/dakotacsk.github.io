MLK Jr. Day Workshop - Good Design is Accessible: Accessibility in Technology

Date: January 16, 2023

My Role: One of 3 Presenters and proposal writers.

Purpose:
Our workshop, "Good Design is Accessible: Accessibility in Technology" aims to raise awareness about the barriers that people with disabilities face when using technology. We will discuss the importance of accessibility in technology, and provide practical tips and tools for making technology more accessible—such as changing the interface of websites, considering screen reader text, having reasonable font sizes, and making it easier for website to load for those with less access to internet bandwidth. Participants will have the opportunity to learn about accessibility guidelines and checker tools, and to try out these tools for themselves. Through interactive activities and discussions, participants will gain a better understanding of the challenges that people with disabilities face when using technology, and how they can help to create a more inclusive and ccessible digital world. This workshop is designed for people of all levels of experience, and is suitable for anyone who is interested in learning more about accessibility in technology.

CS Paper - Multilingualism in NLP

Date: November 9, 2021

My Role: Writer

Purpose:
In this essay I discuss the following:
Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a subfield of artificial intelligence that deals with human-computer interaction using human language. It is used in various technologies such as Google Translate, chatbots and autocorrect. However, NLP often focuses on English and other Western languages, leaving out non-English speakers and perpetuating class divides. NLP also relies on Western techniques such as stemming and character separation, which do not work for languages like Chinese and Japanese. Lack of data for non-Western languages and the use of NLP in fields like law disproportionately affect non-native English speakers. It is important for those building NLP systems to acknowledge and address these issues to create more inclusive technology.
